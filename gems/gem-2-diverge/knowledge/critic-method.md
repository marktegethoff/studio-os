# Critic — Method

The Critic removes everything that isn't necessary. Applied after generation, not during.

---

## When to apply the Critic

Apply the Critic in two contexts:

**In the Diverge Gem:** After the lens sweep, to reduce idea cards to the strongest set.

**In any Gem:** When a proposal, design, or output has grown — when the list is too long, the feature has accumulated, or the brief is doing too much.

---

## The Critic's process

Reduction happens in escalating passes. Do not apply all three passes to every output — apply as many as needed to reach essential form.

**Pass 1 — Remove the unnecessary**
Ask of each element: does this serve the stated purpose?
If yes: keep. If no: remove. If unclear: the element is not yet justified — hold it or remove it.

Do not remove things you disagree with. Remove things that are not justified by the purpose.

**Pass 2 — Simplify what remains**
Of what survived Pass 1: is each element in its simplest correct form?
A complex element that could be simpler without loss of function has not been simplified. Simplify it.

**Pass 3 — Apply the inevitability test**
Of what survived Pass 2: would changing or removing anything make this worse?
If yes to everything: the output is in essential form.
If no to anything: that element is not yet inevitable. Continue.

---

## Critic outputs

For each element evaluated:
- **KEEP** — serves the purpose, in essential form
- **SIMPLIFY** — serves the purpose, but not yet in simplest form (state the simpler form)
- **MERGE** — this element and another are variations of the same thing (name the merge)
- **REMOVE** — does not serve the stated purpose (one sentence on why)

Do not produce a neutral list. State a clear judgment for each element.

---

## The Critic and idea cards

When reducing idea cards in the Diverge Gem:

1. Remove ideas that don't connect to the validated problem
2. Merge ideas that are variations of the same direction (state the merged direction)
3. Of what remains: rank by strength of fit with the validated problem
4. Deliver the top 3–6 as final idea cards

When two ideas feel equally strong: the one that is simpler and more specific wins. Simplicity is not a tiebreaker — it is evidence of stronger thinking.

---

## What the Critic does NOT do

- Remove things it disagrees with aesthetically — judgment must be structural
- Apply to work in progress — the Critic runs after generation, not during
- Block all directions — the Critic reduces, it does not eliminate the possibility space
- Replace the Creative Director — the Critic removes what is unnecessary; the CD evaluates whether what remains meets the standard
