# Lens Prompts

Prompts to activate each ideation lens. Apply only the lenses relevant to the problem. Do not run all lenses by default.

---

## Historical lens

*What has been tried before on this class of problem?*

Prompts:
- What is the underlying problem class, stripped of product-specific framing?
- Which products or systems attempted this problem class in the past decade?
- What specifically did they try?
- Which approaches survived? What made them durable?
- Which failed? What was the specific mechanism of failure — adoption, complexity, trust, scale, or timing?
- Is there a pattern across failures that suggests an enduring constraint?

Output: 2–5 specific examples with outcomes. One-paragraph pattern summary. Explicit note if precedent is sparse.

---

## Adjacent lens

*What does this problem look like in a different domain?*

Prompts:
- Strip the digital/product framing. What is the underlying human problem?
- Where else does this human problem appear? (physical spaces, social contexts, other industries)
- What does the best solution in that domain look like?
- What structural principle makes it work?
- Does that principle transfer to this context? Why or why not?

Output: 1–2 domain transfers. Each names the domain, the structural principle, and why it transfers (or doesn't).

---

## Structural lens

*What is the range of structural solutions?*

Prompts:
- What is the simplest structure that could solve this? (minimum viable structure)
- What is the most complete structure? (full system)
- What are 2–3 points between them?
- What does each structure require of the user?
- What does each structure require of the system?
- Which level of structure matches the current stage of the product and its users?

Output: A spectrum with 3–5 structural positions. Each named, with one sentence on what it requires.

---

## Inversion lens

*What if the constraint were inverted?*

Prompts:
- What is the core assumption being made about how this problem should be solved?
- What if that assumption were false?
- What if the constraint that feels immovable were removed?
- What direction would emerge from that inversion?
- Is that direction interesting? (It usually isn't — but when it is, it's worth pursuing)
- What does the interesting inversion reveal about the original assumption?

Output: 1–2 inversions. Most inversions produce wrong directions — state that clearly and name what the inversion revealed about the original.

---

## Scout lens

*What is the field doing right now?*

Prompts:
- What adjacent products have launched or shipped significant features in this problem area recently?
- What patterns are emerging across multiple products?
- Is there a direction the field is converging on?
- Is there a direction the field has conspicuously avoided? Why?
- What does the field signal suggest about what users are expecting?

Output: 3–5 observations, each tied to a specific product or pattern. No recommendations — report only. The user evaluates strategy.

---

## Using multiple lenses

When using multiple lenses, sequence them:
1. Historical first — to avoid repeating known mistakes
2. Adjacent or Structural second — to generate directions
3. Inversion third — only if the first two feel exhausted
4. Scout last — as a reality check against what's emerging in the world

Not all lenses need to be applied. The right number is the number that generates useful signal. Running a lens that produces nothing is time wasted.
