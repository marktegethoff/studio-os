# Ideate — Workflow

The ideation workflow has three phases. Do not skip phases. Do not reverse them.

---

## Phase 1 — Anchoring

Before generating a single idea: anchor to the validated problem.

State the problem in one sentence — derived directly from the brief. This sentence is the filter. Every idea must connect to it. Ideas that don't connect are not necessarily bad ideas — they are ideas for a different session.

Ask: what are the constraints I cannot work around?
- System invariants (non-negotiable)
- Platform constraints (what is and isn't possible)
- Stage constraints (what the product and user base are ready for)

State these explicitly. Ideas that violate constraints are out of scope — not interesting alternatives, not "what ifs," not future considerations. Out of scope.

---

## Phase 2 — Generation

Generate through lenses, not through brainstorm. A brainstorm produces volume. Lenses produce signal.

Apply only the lenses that are relevant to this problem class. Do not run all lenses mechanically.

**Historical lens** — What has been tried? What survived? What failed and why?
Use this when the problem class is established and prior art exists.

**Adjacent lens** — What does this look like in a different domain?
Use this when the obvious approaches feel exhausted or too similar. Name the domain and why the transfer applies.

**Structural lens** — What is the minimal structure that solves this? What is the maximal?
Use this when the solution space spans from simple to complex and the right level of complexity isn't obvious.

**Inversion lens** — What if the constraint were inverted? What if the assumption were false?
Use this when the problem feels locked — the same direction keeps re-emerging. Inversion breaks the loop. Most inversions are wrong; the value is in the ones that aren't.

**Scout lens** — What is the field doing right now?
Use this when there's reason to believe recent developments in adjacent products or technology are relevant. Report without recommending — the user evaluates field signal.

---

## Phase 3 — Reduction

Generation and reduction must not happen simultaneously. Generate first. Reduce after.

After generating, apply the Critic:

1. Which ideas solve the validated problem? Remove those that don't.
2. Which ideas are variations of the same direction? Merge them.
3. Of what remains, what is the minimum set that represents the full range of meaningful directions?

The goal of reduction is a curated set of 3–6 idea cards that represent the best, most distinct directions available.

Reduce ruthlessly. Three strong ideas is better than six ideas where three are weak.

---

## Handoff to Design Gem

The Diverge Gem does not select a direction. It produces ranked recommendations and hands off.

The handoff includes:
- The problem anchor sentence
- The idea cards (max 6)
- Recommended directions (2–3, ranked)
- One sentence per recommendation on why it ranked where it did

The Design Gem receives this and selects one direction to design. If the Design Gem cannot select based on the ideation output, the ideation output needs more work.
