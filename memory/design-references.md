## Active References

Products and work that actively inform design decisions.

| Product | Observation | Lesson | Stance | Date |
|---------|-------------|--------|--------|------|
| Linear | Keyboard-first, information density matched to expert users, performance as a product decision — fast by design intent not just engineering | Bet on your user and design without compromise for that bet. Density is not a failure; inappropriate density is. | Positive | — |
| Stripe | UX writing integrated into design at the same quality level as visual components — documentation, error messages, onboarding copy | UX writing is not support for design. At its best it is the primary experience layer. | Positive | — |
| Apple Notes | Simple surface, extensive capability accessible but not foregrounded | Don't charge the daily use case for the cost of the edge case. | Positive | — |
| Superhuman | Strong opinionated product philosophy executed with craft — keyboard-first, zero-inbox optimized, every interaction streamlined | Specificity is a design choice. Not the best for everyone is a valid and powerful position. | Positive | — |
| Loom | Friction removal as the primary design work — record, stop, get a link | The most important design work is sometimes what you don't see. Locate and remove every step that is not the behavior itself. | Positive | — |
| Craft (Docs) | Aesthetic quality as explicit product strategy in a commoditized category | In commoditized categories, aesthetic craft is a genuine differentiator. | Positive | — |
| Vercel | Developer experience designed with same intentionality as visual UI — CLI output, build feedback, error messages | Product design extends to every surface the user touches, including surfaces conventionally treated as purely functional. | Positive | — |
| Figma | Collaboration as first-class design capability, not added feature — multiplayer cursors, shared components, real-time feedback | The best features are not improvements to an existing paradigm. They are new paradigms. | Positive | — |
| Arc Browser | Challenged the most settled convention in its category — replaced tabs with spatial sidebar | Willingness to question settled conventions moves conversations forward regardless of market outcome. | Admire-the-move | — |
